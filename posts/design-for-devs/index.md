Long gone are the days, when designers lock themselves in their cubicle to "become inspired", miraculously transform their ideas into a photoshop psd file and throw it over the fence to a developer locked in a dark room expected to turn around a pixel-perfect web site in a couple of days. At least I hope that's not still happening.

Teams are more agile, developers and designers are involved in user research and work even closer together to make sure their product is not only aesthetically pleasing but usable and accessible to all. The design & code cycle should be just that - an iterative cycle that requires frequent collaboration. Here are some tips I've seen from really thoughtful, considerate designers that set me and other developers up for success. Also, some of my design pet-peeves that I hope you'll never make.
